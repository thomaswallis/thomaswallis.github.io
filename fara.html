when i moved to norway i started searching for jobs that would work best for me and would work well with the skills i already had, i found the job at fara as a system engineer as it matched my skills very nicely. fara is a company specialising in bus technologies, so for instance the real time information (my current team) which provides information about the buses location, passenger count, fuel/battery percentage among other information to a central system which then allows the customers to have very detailed information about their busses and how they are doing in the field.

my current responsibilities at fara are as follows:

public displays (bdu), i am soley responsible for these and its quite a tall order as hundreds of thousands of people see these daily across norway so i need to make sure that the code i write and deploy to the devices is reliable and lightweight even the slightest bit of down time reflects negatively on the bus companies and by extension fara.

deployment manager for rto (real time operations), due to my insistance to make sure things are tested on exact customer hardware and software i have become the guy for "front of house" deployments, i make sure that there development team who are working on x branch provide working software that is stable. this has lead to us only having to do 2 software rollbacks for the year of 2022 when the prior year it was 9 and i am working on decreasing the number even further and so far this year it is looking good with only in the first 3 months.

my other responsibility is very general, i work with support tickets from customers to resolve their issues / bugs with our software/hardware and making sure things run hunky dory.
